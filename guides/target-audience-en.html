Finding Your Target Audience

Growth Strategy · April 2026

Download count isn't the only growth metric. Understanding who actually uses, pays for, and recommends your app is the foundation of every ASO and marketing decision.

Know Your Audience

1
Look at your existing users
The most reliable data source is already with you — App Store Connect:

Sales and Trends: Which countries are downloading?
Country breakdown: Where is your most active market?
Paying users: Which market generates revenue?

The country that downloads most and the country that pays most may not be the same — analyze them separately.
In AppConsol, the country breakdown and revenue efficiency views show you which markets are delivering the most value.
2
Build a profile from your reviews
App Store reviews are free user research. When reading reviews, notice:

• What do they praise? Which feature gets mentioned most?
• Who complains? Which problem comes up repeatedly?
• What language do they use? These phrases can become keywords
• Why did they download it? That's your real use case
3
Find out which keywords brought them
App Store Connect → Analytics → Acquisition → Source Type → App Store Search

You can see which search terms led to downloads. This data:

• Shows what users are actually searching for
• Reveals which keywords convert
• Lets you update your metadata strategy with real evidence
High impressions but low conversion means either your screenshots are targeting the wrong audience, or the app doesn't match expectations.

Grow Your Audience

4
Define your user profile
Build a user profile from your data:

Who? Age, occupation, technical level
Where? Geographic distribution
When? Which hours, which days are they active
Why? Which problem are they solving
How? Which feature do they use most

This profile guides your keyword choices, screenshot design, and description.
5
Reach users of similar apps
With Apple Search Ads, you can target competitor app categories. These users:

• Are already active in this category
• Are using another solution but may be dissatisfied
• Have the problem you solve

Bidding on competitor keywords is the fastest way to reach an audience you don't need to capture organically.
6
Find where your best users come from
Look at your paying or most active users — if they share common characteristics, target new people with those same traits.

Questions to ask:
• Which country are they from?
• Which keyword did they use to find you?
• Which feature did they use first?
• How quickly did they convert to premium?

These patterns are the most valuable input to your growth strategy.
Users who convert to premium in the first week are telling you what your app's highest-value moment is. Focus on that moment.
7
Enter new markets through localization
If data shows you're getting organic downloads from a new market but you have no metadata in that language:

Downloads exist → Localize → Visibility increases → More downloads

Localization isn't just translation — you also need to research how users in that market search.
Poor translation can be worse than no translation. Rather than rushing with machine translation, properly localize your core metadata: title, subtitle, and keywords.
