Improve Your ASO Score in 10 Steps

App Store Optimization · April 2026

70% of App Store visitors use search to find new apps — and almost all of them download from the first few results. With the right metadata strategy, you can grow organic visibility without spending on ads.

Metadata Layer

1
Use all 30 characters in your title
Apple gives the title the highest indexing weight of any metadata field. After your app name, add your most important keyword using " — " or ": ".

Example: AppConsol - Sales Analytics (30/30 characters)
Don't repeat title keywords in the keyword field — Apple already indexes them. Use those characters for new terms instead.
2
Don't leave the subtitle empty
The subtitle is 30 characters and fully indexed by Apple. Use it to add secondary keywords that didn't fit in the title.

Bad example: "The best app!"
Good example: "Revenue tracking for app devs"
The subtitle appears in search results — it's critical for both ranking and conversion.
3
Fill the 100-character keyword field completely
The keyword field is iOS-only and invisible to users — it exists purely for Apple's algorithm. Rules:

• Separate with commas, no spaces: tracker,revenue,indie
• Use singular forms (Apple derives plurals automatically)
• Don't repeat words from your title or subtitle
• Aim for 95–100 characters
Every character below 95 is a missed keyword opportunity.
4
Avoid keyword repetition
Apple combines title + subtitle + keyword field into a single index. If a keyword appears in more than one field, it only counts once — and those duplicate characters are wasted.

If your title has "analytics", don't add "analytics" to your keyword field. Spend those 9 characters on a new term.

Localization Layer

5
Research keywords separately for each language
Apple maintains an independent keyword index for each locale. Turkish users search "uygulama analiz" while English users search "app analytics".

Translation alone isn't enough — research what people in that market actually type.
If you're getting downloads from Germany, adding German metadata directly increases your visibility there.
6
Localize for at least English and your top market
An app without localization is nearly invisible in that market. By 2026, 3–5 language localizations is the baseline for competitive apps.

Priority order: EN → your primary market → DE → FR → ES

Behavioral Signals

7
Keep your rating above 4.0
Apple's algorithm considers user ratings alongside download volume. Apps below 4.0 stars see a measurable drop in conversion — no amount of keyword optimization compensates for a poor rating.

The best time to ask for a review: after the user has completed a task successfully, not during onboarding.
Never prompt for a review immediately after onboarding — the user hasn't experienced your app's value yet.
8
Update regularly
Regularly updated apps are treated as "active" by the App Store algorithm. Each update is also an opportunity to revisit your keywords — trends shift.
Every 4–6 weeks, check your keyword rankings. Replace terms that aren't ranking with new candidates.

Conversion Optimization

9
Optimize your first 3 screenshots for conversion
Only the first 1–3 screenshots are visible in search results. These visuals determine whether a user taps your listing at all.

Structure: Problem → Solution → Outcome

Don't include pricing, "#1" claims, or "Best app" language — Apple may reject screenshots with these.
10
Write your description for users, not the algorithm
The App Store description is not indexed by Apple — only users read it. Instead of stuffing keywords, clearly explain what your app does and why it's worth downloading.

The first 3 lines are especially important — that's what users see before tapping "More".
If your app offers auto-renewable subscriptions, you must include a Terms of Use link in the description — Apple requires it.
