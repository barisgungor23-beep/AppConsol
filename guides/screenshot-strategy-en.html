Screenshot Strategy

App Store Optimization · April 2026

70% of App Store users decide whether to tap your listing based on the first 1–3 screenshots — before they even open your app page. These visuals have more impact than your description.

Technical Requirements

Correct screenshot sizes for 2026
Apple simplified things in 2026 — you no longer need to prepare separate sizes for every device.
DeviceSize
iPhone (6.9")1320 × 2868 px
iPhone (6.7")1290 × 2796 px
iPad (13")2064 × 2752 px
Upload the 6.9" size and Apple will automatically scale it for other devices. At minimum, always upload this size.
Technical rules
• Format: PNG (preferred) or JPG
• Color space: RGB, no transparency (alpha channel)
• Maximum 10 screenshots per localized listing
• Portrait for portrait apps, landscape for landscape
• Exact pixel dimensions required — Apple rejects approximate sizes
Don't include pricing ("$0.99", "Free") in screenshots — prices vary by region and Apple may reject them.

Content Strategy

1
Your first screenshot carries your strongest message
In search results, usually only the first screenshot is visible. That single image needs to answer: "What does this app do and why should I care?"

Question format works well: "How much did your app earn today?"
Users make their decision before downloading. The first screenshot is where that decision happens.
2
Use the Problem → Solution → Outcome flow
The highest-converting screenshot sequences tell a mini story:

Screenshot 1: The user's pain point (question format)
Screenshot 2: Your app's solution
Screenshot 3: The concrete benefit or outcome
Screenshots 4–5: Supporting features

Each screenshot builds on the one before it.
3
Show your real app UI
Apple's core rule: screenshots must accurately represent the app.

• Show real app UI — not conceptual designs
• Every feature shown must exist in the submitted version
• If showing IAP-gated features, indicate this clearly
• Text overlays and device frames are allowed
When you update your app, update your screenshots too. Showing outdated UI is one of the most common rejection reasons.
4
Keep text large and minimal
Screenshots appear small in search results. Your text should:

• Be large enough to read as a thumbnail (test it at small size)
• Headline: maximum 4–5 words
• Subtext: maximum 1 line if used at all
• Dark text on light backgrounds, white text on dark
5
Localize screenshots for each language
Having a localized description with English-only screenshots hurts conversion. Apple also treats this as low effort.

At minimum, localize the text in your first 3 screenshots.
Write screenshot text with localization in mind from the start — retrofitting is harder.

Common Mistakes

What not to do
• Including pricing: "$0.99" or "Free" — prices vary by region, rejection risk

• "#1" or "Best" claims: Unverified superlatives are rejected by Apple

• Raw screenshots without design: No background, frame, or text — converts poorly

• Same message on every screen: Each screenshot should deliver a distinct value proposition

• Not updating screenshots after an app update: One of the most common rejection reasons
