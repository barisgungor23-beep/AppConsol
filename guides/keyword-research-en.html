Keyword Research

App Store Optimization · April 2026

70% of App Store searches result in a direct download. Finding the right keywords is the foundation of organic growth — without ad spend.

Core Concepts

How does the App Store keyword system work?
Apple indexes keywords from three fields:

1. Title (30 characters): Highest indexing weight
2. Subtitle (30 characters): Second highest weight
3. Keyword field (100 characters): Additional keywords, invisible to users

The description is not indexed. Each language has an independent index.
Apple combines words across these three fields to index multi-word searches. You don't need to enter "revenue tracker" as a phrase — adding "revenue" and "tracker" separately is enough.

Step-by-Step Research

1
Build a seed keyword list
Start from your user's perspective: "What would someone type into App Store search to find this app?"

List 20–30 candidate keywords:
• Category names for your app
• The problem it solves
• The technology or platform you use
• Competitor app names
• Terms your target audience uses
2
Use App Store autocomplete
The cheapest and most up-to-date keyword research: type a word into the App Store search box and watch the suggestions.

Autocomplete suggestions are based on real user searches. Typing "analytics" shows you what people are actually looking for.
Repeat this process in different languages — users in different markets use different terms.
3
Analyze competitor keywords
Look at the titles and subtitles of top-ranking apps in your category:

• Which keywords are they using?
• What's in their titles that's missing from yours?
• Which words appear repeatedly — that's a signal of high traffic

In App Store Connect's "Search" tab, you can track your own keyword rankings.
4
Prioritize your keywords
For each keyword, ask:

Traffic: How many people search for this?
Competition: How many apps are competing for it?
Relevance: Is someone searching this my actual user?

Best keyword: high traffic + low competition + high relevance

Focus on long-tail keywords: "app revenue tracker" is less competitive and more specific than "sales analytics".
5
Place keywords in the right fields
Title: Your 1–2 most important keywords
Subtitle: 2–3 secondary keywords
Keyword field: All remaining keywords, comma-separated, no spaces

Use singular forms — Apple derives plurals automatically.
Skip stopwords ("and", "for", "the") — Apple doesn't index them anyway.
Don't repeat words from your title or subtitle in the keyword field — every repeated character is wasted space.
6
Review every 4–6 weeks
Keyword optimization is not a one-time task. The App Store algorithm changes, competitors update, user behavior shifts.

• Which keywords am I ranking highly for?
• Which keywords am I invisible for?
• Are there new trends to capture?

Remove low-ranking keywords and replace them with new candidates.
AppConsol's ASO score measures keyword freshness — it shows how long it's been since your last keyword update.

Common Mistakes

What to avoid
• Keyword stuffing in the title: Meaningless keyword piles — Apple rejects them and users bounce

• Using competitor app names: Apple can detect and remove these

• Only thinking in English: Each language requires its own research

• High-traffic but irrelevant keywords: Brings clicks but not downloads — hurts conversion rate

• Never updating: Keywords not updated in 3+ months tend to drop in rankings
